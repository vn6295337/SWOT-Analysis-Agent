# SEC EDGAR Fundamentals - Business Guide

A tool that fetches official financial data from SEC filings for SWOT analysis.

## What It Provides

| Data Category | Metrics | What It Tells You |
|---------------|---------|-------------------|
| **Profitability** | Revenue, Net Income, Margins | Company's ability to generate and retain profits |
| **Growth** | Revenue CAGR (3-year) | Business momentum and market demand |
| **Leverage** | Debt, Debt-to-Equity | Financial risk and capital structure |
| **Cash Flow** | Operating CF, Free Cash Flow | Ability to fund operations and investments |
| **Investment** | R&D Expense, CapEx | Future growth potential |

## How to Interpret

### Revenue Growth (3-Year CAGR)

| Growth Rate | Performance | What It Means for SWOT |
|-------------|-------------|------------------------|
| Above 15% | High growth | Strength - strong market demand |
| 5% - 15% | Moderate growth | Strength - healthy expansion |
| 0% - 5% | Slow growth | Neutral - stable but limited |
| Below 0% | Declining | Weakness - shrinking business |

### Net Profit Margin

| Margin | Profitability | What It Means for SWOT |
|--------|---------------|------------------------|
| Above 15% | Highly profitable | Strength - pricing power, efficiency |
| 5% - 15% | Healthy | Strength - sustainable business |
| 0% - 5% | Thin margins | Weakness - vulnerable to cost increases |
| Below 0% | Unprofitable | Weakness - losing money |

### Debt-to-Equity Ratio

| Ratio | Leverage Level | What It Means for SWOT |
|-------|----------------|------------------------|
| Below 0.5 | Conservative | Strength - financial flexibility |
| 0.5 - 1.0 | Moderate | Neutral - balanced capital structure |
| 1.0 - 2.0 | Elevated | Weakness - higher financial risk |
| Above 2.0 | High leverage | Threat - significant debt burden |

### Free Cash Flow

| Status | Cash Generation | What It Means for SWOT |
|--------|-----------------|------------------------|
| Positive (growing) | Strong | Strength - self-funding, dividend capacity |
| Positive (stable) | Adequate | Neutral - meeting obligations |
| Negative | Cash burn | Weakness - needs external funding |

## Example: Apple (AAPL)

| Metric | Value | Interpretation | SWOT Category |
|--------|-------|----------------|---------------|
| Revenue | $383B | Massive scale | Strength |
| Net Margin | 25% | Highly profitable | Strength |
| Debt/Equity | 1.8x | Elevated leverage | Weakness |
| Free Cash Flow | $99B | Exceptional | Strength |
| R&D % | 7% | Significant investment | Opportunity |

**Summary:** Apple demonstrates strong profitability and cash generation, though carries elevated debt. High R&D investment suggests continued innovation potential.

## Data Source

All data sourced from **SEC EDGAR** - the official repository of company filings:
- 10-K (Annual Reports)
- 10-Q (Quarterly Reports)
- XBRL structured financial data

This is the same data used by institutional investors and analysts.

## Material Events (8-K Filings)

8-K filings disclose material corporate events. High-priority events for risk assessment:

| Event                 | What It Means                          | SWOT Impact                           |
| --------------------- | -------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Bankruptcy Filing     | Company entering bankruptcy protection | Threat - severe financial distress    |
| Material Impairment   | Asset writedowns                       | Weakness - declining asset values     |
| Delisting Notice      | Exchange listing at risk               | Threat - liquidity/reputation risk    |
| Financial Restatement | Prior financials unreliable            | Threat - accounting/governance issues |
| Change in Control     | Ownership change                       | Weakness - strategic uncertainty      |
| Executive Departure   | Key leader leaving                     | Weakness - leadership risk            |

## Use Cases

- **Due diligence**: Verify company financials from official sources
- **Competitor analysis**: Compare financial metrics across peers
- **Trend analysis**: Track multi-year performance
- **Risk assessment**: Evaluate leverage and cash position
- **Event monitoring**: Track material corporate events (8-K)
